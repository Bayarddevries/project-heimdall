CAN-057 — Kensington Lights (2014)

June 4, 2014. John Sheppard witnessed unusual lights over the Gulf of St. Lawrence while putting out a bonfire. Captured 22 minutes of video on his cellphone. MUFON confirmed the sighting. CBC News covered it with follow-up analysis offering alternate explanations. Only PEI case.

📍 Kensington, PEI · 🕐 Night · 🔭 NL · 📱 Cellphone video · 📰 CBC
BC Tier C HIGH

CAN-058 — Trail River Lights (1997)

July 1, 1997 (Canada Day). Two women on the Columbia River bridge, waiting for fireworks, saw three small lights above them. The lights descended to 1-2 feet above the water, zig-zagged eastbound along the river, and flew out of sight. Still daylight — fireworks hadn't started. Two independent witnesses who were strangers to each other.

📍 Trail, BC · 🕐 Evening · 🔭 NL · 🌉 Columbia River · 🎆 Canada Day
ON Tier C

CAN-059 — North York Flashing Lights (2014)

July 26, 2014, 7-10 PM. Multiple reports of a string of 6-7 diagonal flashing lights over North York, Toronto. Sarah Chun recorded two videos from her condo balcony (posted to YouTube). Toronto Police 32 Division received several calls. Multiple police officers witnessed the lights and one speculated it was a quadcopter (but didn't investigate).

📍 North York (Toronto), ON · 🕐 Evening · 🔭 NL · 🎥 YouTube · 👮 Police witnesses

Includes the Carman (CAN-023) and Portage la Prairie (CAN-028) incidents, plus additional unreported cases. Chris Rutkowski / Ufology Research of Manitoba documented the flap extensively.

📍 Southern MB · 📅 Multi-month · 🔭 Multiple types · 👮 RCMP reports

CAN-062 — 1989 Montreal Mass Sighting

November 1989. Thousands of witnesses across Montreal reported a massive UFO hovering over the city. Described as a large boomerang-shaped craft with lights along its edges. Lasted approximately one hour. Covered extensively in Montreal media. One of the largest mass sightings in Canadian history — currently not represented in any HEIMDALL case.

📍 Montreal, QC · 🔭 NL/CE1 · 👥 Thousands of witnesses · 📰 Mass media
