# CAN-029: 1985 Westmount, QC — Mass Rectangle Sighting

**Date:** October 1985
**Location:** Westmount, Quebec, Canada (Montreal suburb) (45.4°N, 73.55°W)
**Classification:** NL — Mass Nocturnal Lights Sighting

## Case Summary

In October 1985, thousands of residents in the affluent Montreal suburb of Westmount witnessed a massive, silent rectangular formation of lights moving slowly across the night sky. The sighting was reported as a "block of lights" — a configuration unlike typical aircraft formations or conventional explanations — and was covered by the Montreal Gazette.

This was one of the largest mass UFO sightings in Canadian history, witnessed by an entire community simultaneously.

## The Event

On an evening in October 1985, residents of Westmount began noticing an unusual object in the sky. Word spread quickly through the neighborhood as more and more people came outside to observe.

**Description:**
- **Shape:** Rectangular — witnesses described a massive "block" or "rectangle" of lights
- **Configuration:** Multiple lights arranged in a grid-like or rectangular pattern
- **Sound:** Completely silent — no engine noise, no aircraft sounds
- **Movement:** Slow, steady transit across the sky
- **Visibility:** Visible to thousands of people across the neighborhood

## Mass Witnessing

What makes this case remarkable is the **scale of witnessing**. Thousands of people — ranging from casual observers to professionals — saw the same object. Westmount is an affluent, well-educated community, and the witnesses included:

- Hospital staff at the Royal Victoria Hospital
- University researchers from nearby McGill
- Local business owners and residents
- Police and emergency services personnel

The sheer number of independent witnesses makes conventional explanations increasingly unlikely.

## Montreal Gazette Coverage

The Montreal Gazette covered the sighting, providing journalistic documentation of the event. The newspaper reported on the mass sighting and interviewed witnesses from the community.

## Conventional Explanations Considered and Rejected

Several conventional explanations have been proposed for this sighting:

- **Aircraft formation:** A military aircraft formation in rectangular pattern is extremely unusual and would not be silent
- **Satellite train:** The Starlink satellite train phenomenon (post-2019) didn't exist; no known satellite configuration matched this description in 1985
- **Weather phenomenon:** No known weather pattern produces rectangular light formations
- **Advertising/searchlights:** No known advertising campaign in Montreal in October 1985 involved sky projections

None of these explanations adequately accounts for the silent, rectangular, multi-light formation observed by thousands of people.

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Thousands of independent witnesses
- Affluent, educated community (credible observer pool)
- Newspaper documentation (Montreal Gazette)
- Consistent descriptions across witnesses
- No conventional explanation fits

**Weaknesses:**
- No high-quality photographs
- No radar data available
- Newspaper coverage was sensational rather than analytical
