# CAN-039: 2007 St-Hilaire, QC — Orb Rising from Mont St-Hilaire

**Date:** 2007
**Location:** Mont-Saint-Hilaire, Quebec, Canada (45.5°N, 73.1°W)
**Classification:** NL — Nocturnal Lights | Orb Emergence from Mountain

## Case Summary

In 2007, multiple residents of the Mont-Saint-Hilaire area in Quebec's Montérégie region observed a glowing luminous orb that appeared to emerge from within or behind the mountain itself. The event was reported by local residents and documented through community channels.

## Geographic Context — Mont-Saint-Hilaire

Mont-Saint-Hilaire (also known as Mont Saint-Hilaire or simply St-Hilaire) is one of the nine Montérégie Hills:
- Located south of Montreal in Quebec's Montérégie region
- A prominent landmark visible for miles across the flat St. Lawrence lowlands
- The mountain is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve (designated in 1978)
- Home to a nature reserve with protected forest ecosystems
- The community of Mont-Saint-Hilaire sits at the base of the mountain

The mountain's isolated, prominent nature makes it an ideal reference point for sky observations. An object emerging from behind or within the mountain would be clearly visible to residents in the surrounding area.

## The Event

**Observation Details:**
- **Object:** A glowing orb — a spherical luminous object
- **Behavior:** The orb appeared to emerge from the mountain — either rising from behind it or appearing to come from within it
- **Visibility:** Observed by multiple local residents across the community
- **Time:** Nighttime/low-light conditions (the orb was glowing, suggesting darkness)

## Local Response

The sighting was reported through local community channels:
- Multiple residents reported the observation
- The event generated community discussion
- It was documented in Quebec regional UFO databases

## Montérégie UFO Context

The Montérégie region (south of Montreal, including St-Hilaire, St-Jean-sur-Richelieu, and surrounding communities) has produced several notable UFO reports:
- **1994 (CAN-036):** St-Jean-sur-Richelieu trace case with dehydrated vegetation (ground effects)
- **2007 (CAN-039):** St-Hilaire orb emerging from mountain (this case)

The concentration of reports in this region may reflect:
- Proximity to Montreal (high population density = more observers)
- The clear sightlines provided by the flat St. Lawrence lowlands and prominent Montérégie Hills
- Community awareness and willingness to report
