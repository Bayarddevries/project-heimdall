# CAN-035: 1993 Port Colborne, ON — The Missing Time Case

**Date:** 1993
**Location:** Port Colborne, Ontario, Canada (42.8°N, 79.2°W)
**Classification:** CE4 — Close Encounter of the Fourth Kind (Abduction/Missing Time)

## Case Summary

In 1993, a single witness in Port Colborne, Ontario experienced a bright light encounter followed by a significant period of missing time. The event was subsequently investigated through hypnotic regression sessions, during which the witness recovered memories of the encounter.

## The Event

What is known:
- The witness observed a bright light in close proximity
- Following the encounter, a period of missing time was discovered — several hours the witness could not account for
- The witness sought investigation of the missing time through hypnotic regression

## Hypnotic Regression

Professional hypnotic regression sessions were conducted:
- The witness recovered detailed memories of the encounter
- Entities were described during the regression
- The account was consistent across multiple regression sessions

## Context — Lake Erie

Port Colborne sits on the shores of Lake Erie, which has a history of anomalous activity:
- The 2004 Fort Erie "vibrating lights" case (CAN-039) occurred in the same region
- Lake Erie's shoreline (both Canadian and American sides) has a concentration of reports

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Detailed regression account
- Consistent testimony
- Consistent with regional activity pattern (Lake Erie area)

**Weaknesses:**
- Single witness (no corroboration)
- Hypnotic regression evidence is controversial
- No physical evidence
- CE4 cases are inherently difficult to verify objectively
