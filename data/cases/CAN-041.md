# CAN-041: 2024 Hafford, SK — Domed Disc Over Farmland

**Date:** 2024
**Location:** Hafford, Saskatchewan (52.8°N, 107.3°W)
**Classification:** CE1 — Close Encounter (Visual Sighting)

## Case Summary

In 2024, a witness in Hafford, Saskatchewan reported observing a domed disc-shaped craft at low altitude hovering over rural farmland. The sighting was formally filed as MUFON Case #13582, adding it to the organization's international database of UFO reports.

This is one of the most recent entries in the HEIMDALL database and represents a modern-era sighting of a classic "saucer" shape.

## The Observation

The witness described a domed disc-shaped object — the iconic "flying saucer" shape that has been reported since 1947 — hovering at low altitude over agricultural land near Hafford. Key details:

- **Shape:** Domed disc — the classic "saucer" configuration with a raised dome on top
- **Altitude:** Low — hovering above farmland, visible in detail
- **Duration:** Sufficient for the witness to observe the domed structure clearly
- **Movement:** Controlled hovering over the field

## MUFON Documentation

The sighting was reported to MUFON (Mutual UFO Network) and assigned case number 13582. MUFON's reporting system requires structured documentation of:
- Date, time, and location
- Detailed object description
- Environmental conditions
- Witness information and credibility assessment

The case was included in the Canadian UFO Survey's 2024 compilation of notable sightings.

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Classic domed disc description (iconic shape with high recognition factor)
- Filed with MUFON through their structured reporting system
- Witness was confident enough in the observation to file a formal report

**Weaknesses:**
- Single witness only
- No physical evidence, photographs, or video
- No corroborating witnesses or radar data
- One of the most recent cases — investigation is still fresh

## Agent Note
This case is important for the "modern continuity" theme of HEIMDALL. The fact that a classic domed disc was reported in rural Saskatchewan in 2024 — nearly 80 years after the Kenneth Arnold sighting — shows that these reports haven't stopped. The shape descriptions remain consistent across decades, which is one of the most persistent mysteries in the UFO phenomenon.
