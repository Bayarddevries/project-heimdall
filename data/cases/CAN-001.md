# CAN-001: 1662 Montreal — Fiery Serpents in the Sky

**Date:** 1662
**Location:** Montreal, New France (Ville-Marie settlement) (45.5°N, 73.5°W)
**Classification:** NL — Nocturnal Lights | Earliest Recorded Canadian UFO Sighting

## Case Summary

In 1662, the colonists and Jesuit missionaries of the fledgling settlement of Ville-Marie (modern Montreal) witnessed an extraordinary aerial phenomenon: objects described as "fiery serpents" in the sky above the settlement. This is the earliest documented UFO-like sighting in Canadian history, predating the modern UFO era by nearly 300 years.

The event was recorded in the Jesuit Relations — the annual reports sent by Jesuit missionaries in New France back to their superiors in Paris.

## Historical Context — 1662 New France

The colony of New France in 1662 was a precarious settlement:
- Ville-Marie (Montreal) was barely 20 years old, founded in 1642
- The settlement was under constant threat from Haudenosaunee (Iroquois) forces
- Life was harsh, survival uncertain
- The Jesuit missionaries maintained detailed records of all events, natural and otherwise

In this context, an unexplained aerial phenomenon would have been interpreted through the religious lens of the era — as an omen, a divine sign, or a supernatural event.

## The Event

**Description from the Jesuit Relations:**

The colonists and missionaries reported seeing "fiery serpents" in the sky above the settlement. The objects were described as serpentine — elongated, luminous, and apparently alive in their movement (writhing).

The description is notable for several reasons:

1. **The term "serpent":** In 17th-century colonial vocabulary, "serpent" would have been the best available metaphor for an elongated, undulating object moving through the sky. It implies shape, movement, and a sense of agency.

2. **Multiple witnesses:** The event was observed by multiple settlers and the educated Jesuit priests, providing corroborating testimony.

3. **Contemporaneous documentation:** The Jesuit Relations were formal annual reports intended for their superiors in Paris. The priests had no reason to fabricate or exaggerate — they were simply recording what they and their congregants witnessed.

## Interpreting "Fiery Serpents"

Modern researchers have offered various interpretations:

- **Unusual atmospheric phenomenon:** Aurora borealis or unusual cloud formations, though the "writhing" description makes aurora unlikely
- **Meteor shower or fireball:** Multiple meteors entering the atmosphere, though the sustained "serpentine" behavior doesn't match typical meteor descriptions
- **Unidentified aerial vehicles:** Proponents of the UFO hypothesis note that the description matches modern reports of elongated, luminous craft with unusual movement patterns
- **Religious interpretation (contemporary):** The colonists and Jesuits interpreted it as a divine omen — a celestial sign meant for the settlement

## Significance

The 1662 Montreal event is significant because:

- It is the **earliest documented UFO-like sighting in Canada**
- It predates the Kenneth Arnold sighting (1947) by 285 years
- It demonstrates that such phenomena have been observed throughout human history, regardless of era or culture
- The documentation is contemporaneous and formal (not a folk tale or oral tradition)

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Contemporaneous written documentation in formal records
- Multiple independent witnesses (settlers + educated Jesuit priests)
- No apparent motive for fabrication
- Specific descriptive details (shape, movement)

**Weaknesses:**
- Interpretation colored by 17th-century religious worldview
- No physical evidence beyond the written record
- Description ("fiery serpents") is metaphorical and subject to interpretation
