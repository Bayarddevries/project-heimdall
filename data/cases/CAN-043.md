# CAN-043: 2014 Vancouver, BC — YVR Airport Near-Miss

**Date:** 2014
**Location:** Vancouver International Airport (YVR) airspace, British Columbia (49.2°N, 123.1°W)
**Classification:** DD — Daylight Disc | Aviation Safety Near-Miss

## Case Summary

In 2014, a commercial airline pilot operating in and around Vancouver International Airport (YVR) encountered an unidentified mechanical object on a potential collision course with the passenger aircraft. The incident was significant enough to trigger a near-miss report and was documented in Transport Canada's CADORS (Civil Aviation Daily Occurrence Report) system.

## CADORS

CADORS is Transport Canada's system for documenting aviation safety occurrences:
- All significant aviation events are reported, including near-misses, mechanical issues, and unidentified objects in airspace
- CADORS reports are official government documents
- A CADORS filing for a UFO encounter means the pilot took the threat seriously enough to file a mandatory safety report

## The Incident

What is known:
- A commercial pilot on approach or departure from YVR observed an unidentified mechanical object
- The object was on a trajectory that required pilot awareness and potentially evasive action
- The object was described as "mechanical" — structured, manufactured, not a natural phenomenon
- The encounter occurred in the busy YVR airspace, one of Canada's busiest airports

## Aviation Significance

This case is different from most UFO reports:
- It was reported through official aviation safety channels
- The observer was a commercial airline pilot (highly trained, operating under strict protocols)
- The object posed a potential aviation safety hazard
- Near-miss reports trigger investigation and follow-up

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Commercial airline pilot (most credible observer category possible)
- CADORS official filing (government aviation safety documentation)
- YVR — one of Canada's busiest airports
- Potential aviation safety hazard (object on collision course)

**Weaknesses:**
- No ATC radar data confirmed for the object
- Limited public CADORS detail
- No public photograph or video from the cockpit
