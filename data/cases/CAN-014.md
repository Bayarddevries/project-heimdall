# CAN-014: 1967 St. Paul, AB — The UFO Landing Pad

**Date:** 1967
**Location:** St. Paul, Alberta, Canada (53.9°N, 111.3°W)
**Classification:** Cultural Event — Not a sighting; community phenomenon

## Case Summary

In 1967, the town of St. Paul, Alberta made a decision that would make it unique in the world: they built a UFO landing pad. As a centennial project celebrating Canada's 100th birthday, the town council approved the construction of a 60-foot diameter concrete UFO landing pad — the only such structure in the world officially designated for visiting spacecraft.

## The Landing Pad

**Specifications:**
- **Size:** 60-foot (approximately 18 meter) diameter concrete pad
- **Location:** On the outskirts of town, accessible to visitors
- **Design:** Concrete circular pad with a central tower
- **Tower:** Includes a beacon tower, approximately 30 feet tall
- **Plaque:** Dedicated as a landing area for extraterrestrial spacecraft
- **Status:** Still standing today; a tourist attraction

## The Decision

The St. Paul town council's reasoning was reportedly pragmatic and playful:
- If flying saucers are visiting Earth, they should have a designated place to land
- The landing pad would serve as a permanent landmark for the town
- It was an affordable centennial project with lasting impact
- It generated national and international publicity

## Cultural Significance

The St. Paul UFO Landing Pad is:
- The **only official UFO landing pad in the world**
- A **registered tourist attraction** in Alberta
- A symbol of 1960s UFO culture intersecting with municipal governance
- Evidence of how seriously (or playfully) Canadian communities took the UFO phenomenon

The town of St. Paul continues to celebrate its UFO connection to this day.

## Assessment

This case is classified as "Cultural" rather than a standard sighting. It documents a community's formal acknowledgment of and response to the UFO phenomenon.
