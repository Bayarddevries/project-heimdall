# CAN-010: 1966 Agassiz, BC — The Light Ship over Mount Cheam

**Date:** 1966
**Location:** Agassiz, British Columbia (near Mount Cheam) (49.2°N, 121.7°W)
**Classification:** NL — Nocturnal Lights | Luminous Craft

## Case Summary

In 1966, multiple residents of the Agassiz/Harrison community in British Columbia's Fraser Valley reported observing a luminous craft hovering over Mount Cheam, a prominent peak in the Cascade Mountains visible from the Agassiz area. The sighting became a local legend and was documented by community members.

## Geographic Context — Mount Cheam and Agassiz

Agassiz sits in the Fraser Valley, one of BC's most populated agricultural corridors. Mount Cheam is a prominent landmark:
- Mount Cheam rises to 2,161 meters (7,090 feet)
- It is the dominant peak visible from Harrison Hot Springs and Agassiz
- The mountain has First Nations cultural significance
- Weather conditions are well understood by local residents

A luminous object hovering at the peak would be visible from the entire valley floor.

## The Event

**Witness Description:**
- **Object:** Described as a "light ship" — a luminous craft emitting bright light
- **Position:** Hovering over or near the peak of Mount Cheam
- **Movement:** Stationary hover; no typical aircraft movement
- **Duration:** Extended period; multiple witnesses observed over time
- **Conditions:** Nighttime observation; clear enough for the object to be distinctly visible

## Community Response

The sighting generated significant community interest in Agassiz/Harrison:
- Multiple local residents reported the sighting
- It became part of local lore and was remembered and discussed in the community
- Local press covered the event
- The case entered BC UFO research files

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Multiple local witnesses
- Prominent, familiar landmark (no uncertainty about location)
- Consistent descriptions across witnesses
- Community documentation and local press coverage

**Weaknesses:**
- No physical evidence beyond witness accounts
- Some details may have been embellished through oral retelling
- "Light ship" description is somewhat vague
