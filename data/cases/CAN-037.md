# CAN-037: 1996 Fox Lake, YT — The Mothership

**Date:** 1996
**Location:** Fox Lake, Yukon Territory, Canada (61.2°N, 135.4°W)
**Classification:** CE1 — Close Encounter | Mass Community Sighting

## Case Summary

In 1996, a massive craft estimated at approximately 3 football fields wide (roughly 300 meters) was observed by 31 witnesses over Fox Lake, Yukon Territory. The event was documented by the Yukon Government and represents one of the largest-mass UFO sightings in Canadian history, with the added credibility of an enormous craft seen by dozens of people in the pristine northern sky.

## The Event

**Observation Details:**
- **Size:** Approximately 300 meters wide (3 football fields) — this is the size of a city block
- **Shape:** Described as a "mothership" — a massive craft with an extensive light configuration
- **Altitude:** Low enough for witnesses to estimate its enormous size
- **Movement:** Slow, deliberate movement over the Fox Lake area
- **Duration:** Long enough for 31 people to observe and report

## The Witness Pool

The 31 witnesses included:
- Fox Lake community residents
- First Nations community members
- Possibly visiting workers or travelers

In a small Yukon community, 31 witnesses represents a significant percentage of the local population, making this effectively a community-wide event.

## Fox Lake, YT Context

Fox Lake is:
- A small First Nations community in the southern Yukon
- Remote; minimal air traffic
- Clear northern sky provides excellent visibility
- Residents are experienced outdoors people who know what aircraft look like

## Significance

The Fox Lake case is significant because:
- **Scale of craft:** A 300-meter craft is enormous — larger than any conventional aircraft
- **Witness count:** 31 people is one of the highest witness counts for any Canadian UFO case
- **Northern location:** Eliminates common misidentification factors
- **Government documentation:** Yukon Government files confirm the reports

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- 31 witnesses — one of the largest witnessed UFO events in Canada
- Massive craft size (300 meters) rules out conventional aircraft
- Remote location (no misidentification risk)
- Government documentation (Yukon files)
- Community-wide event

**Weaknesses:**
- No photographs or video
- No radar data
- No physical evidence

## Classification
- Hynek System: CE1 (Close Encounter - Visual)
- Credibility Rating: HIGH (31 witnesses; massive craft; remote location; government documentation)
- Case Status: UNRESOLVED — No conventional aircraft matches the 300-meter scale.
