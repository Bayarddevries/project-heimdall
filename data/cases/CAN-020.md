# CAN-020: 1973 Granby, QC — Quebec Wave Close Encounter

**Date:** 1973
**Location:** Granby, Quebec, Canada (45.4°N, 72.73°W)
**Classification:** CE3 — Close Encounter of the Third Kind (Entity Contact)

## Case Summary

During the early 1970s Quebec UFO wave — a period of intense unusual activity across the province — the Granby area experienced multiple close encounters, including reports of small humanoid beings and physical trace evidence. The Granby cases are part of a broader pattern of Quebec UFO activity that would intensify throughout the 1970s.

## Historical Context — The Quebec UFO Wave

Quebec was one of the most active regions for UFO sightings in Canada during the 1970s:

- The province has consistently produced some of the most detailed UFO reports in Canadian
- French-Canadian witnesses often provided more detailed descriptions of entities and craft
- GRUPI (Groupe de Recherches sur les UPI), a Quebec-based UFO research group, maintained
 extensive documentation
- The wave of activity in the 1970s set the stage for the even more famous 1975 wave (with cases like the Val Solange encounter)

The Quebec wave was characterized by:
- High numbers of close encounters (CE3 and CE4 category)
- Entity reports (small beings, tall beings, and various humanoid types)
- Physical trace evidence (ground burns, electromagnetic interference)
- Mass community awareness and reporting

## The Granby Encounters

**Witness Reports:**
Multiple witnesses in the Granby area reported encounters with unidentified craft and, in some cases, the beings associated with them.

**Craft Descriptions:**
- Various shapes reported: disc, oval, and spherical
- Silent operation
- Multi-colored lights
- Hovering capability with sudden rapid acceleration
- Low-altitude operation (tree-top to rooftop level)

**Entity Reports:**
Some witnesses reported seeing small humanoid beings in association with the craft. These reports are consistent with the "small being" theme that appeared across multiple Quebec wave encounters.

**Physical Evidence:**
- Ground trace evidence reported in the area
- Electromagnetic interference with vehicles and electronics (consistent with the broader Quebec wave pattern)

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Consistent reports across multiple witnesses
- Physical trace evidence (ground marks, EM interference)
- Part of a documented regional wave pattern
- Entity sightings with consistent descriptions
- French-Canadian witness pool (known for detailed reporting)

**Weaknesses:**
- Entity reports are inherently subjective
- Some reports may be cross-contaminated by awareness of other Quebec wave cases
- Documentation quality varies between reports
