# CAN-015: 1968 Rivers, MB — Glowing Ring Radar Track

**Date:** 1968
**Location:** Rivers, Manitoba, Canada (50.0°N, 100.2°W)
**Classification:** RV — Radar-Visual Detection

## Case Summary

In 1968 — eighteen years after the 1950 RCAF disc encounter in the same area (CAN-004) — Air Traffic Control personnel at the Rivers, Manitoba area detected a glowing ring-shaped object on radar. The object maintained a persistent radar track, suggesting a sustained presence rather than a brief flash or anomaly.

## Historical Context — Rivers, Manitoba

Rivers, Manitoba has an unusual distinction in Canadian UFO history:

- **1950 (CAN-004):** RCAF pilot encountered a metallic disc — high-speed maneuvers
- **1968 (CAN-015):** ATC detected a glowing ring — persistent radar track

The fact that two significant, radar-confirmed UFO events occurred in the same general area over an 18-year span is noteworthy and may suggest a pattern of recurring activity.

## The Event

**Detection Details:**
- **Shape:** Described as a "glowing ring" — the object appeared to have a ring-like configuration
- **Confirmation:** Detected and tracked on radar equipment
- **Duration:** Persistent — the radar track was sustained over a period of time
- **Witnesses:** Air Traffic Control personnel (trained radar operators)

## Air Traffic Control Context

ATC personnel are trained to identify and categorize all objects on their radar screens:
- They can distinguish between commercial aircraft, military aircraft, weather returns, and anomalies
- A "persistent radar track" means the object maintained a steady, trackable course
- The glowing ring description suggests visual confirmation as well as radar

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Trained ATC observers
- Radar equipment confirmation
- Persistent (sustained) track
- Second significant event in the same area as CAN-004

**Weaknesses:**
- No physical evidence beyond radar data
- Limited public documentation
- The "glowing ring" description is unusual and may represent a specific type of craft or phenomenon

## Agent Notes
The Rivers, MB cases (CAN-004 in 1950 and CAN-015 in 1968) form a pattern. Same geographic area, 18 years apart, both radar-confirmed. This is one of the few cases of recurring activity at a specific Canadian location.
