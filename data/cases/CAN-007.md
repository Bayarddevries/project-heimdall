# CAN-007: 1954 Ennadai Lake, NU — The Metallic Cylinder

**Date:** 1954
**Location:** Ennadai Lake, Nunavut, Canada (61.2°N, 100.9°W)
**Classification:** DD — Daylight Disc | Arctic Weather Station Observation

## Case Summary

Weather observers at a remote Arctic station near Ennadai Lake logged a metallic cylindrical object moving through the sky with no visible means of propulsion. The sighting was documented in DND files and later declassified.

## Historical Context — Ennadai Lake

Ennadai Lake sits in the heart of the Canadian Arctic:
- In 1954, this was a remote military weather observation station
- The clarity of Arctic skies and absence of normal air traffic made unusual objects immediately conspicuous
- Military observers stationed there were trained to document all aerial phenomena

## The Sighting

**Observation Details:**
- **Shape:** Metallic cylinder — a tubular, elongated form
- **Surface:** Metallic with visible glint in sunlight
- **Propulsion:** None observed — no wings, engine exhaust, or contrails
- **Weather observers' interpretation:** The witnesses could not identify the object as any known aircraft or natural phenomenon

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Trained military weather observers
- Arctic clarity (no atmospheric distortion)
- DND declassified documentation
- Object identified specifically as artificial (metallic, structured)

**Weaknesses:**
- Single location observation
- No photographs
- Remote location limited investigation

## Classification
- Hynek System: DD (Daylight Disc)
- Credibility Rating: HIGH (trained observers; DND documented)
- Case Status: UNRESOLVED — No conventional explanation for the propulsionless metallic cylinder
