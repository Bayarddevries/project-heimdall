# CAN-019: Val-d'Or 1973 — Quebec UFO Wave

**Date:** August-October 1973
**Location:** Val-d'Or, Quebec and the Abitibi-Témiscamingue region (48.1°N, 77.7°W)
**Classification:** CE1 — Mass Encounter / Regional Wave

## Case Summary

In the late summer and fall of 1973, the Val-d'Or mining region of Quebec experienced a sustained wave of UFO sightings that lasted several weeks. Hundreds of witnesses — including police officers, mining workers, and civilian residents — reported seeing multi-colored luminous objects performing extraordinary aerial maneuvers over the Abitibi region.

This was part of what Quebec ufologists called "La Vague de 1973" — the 1973 UFO Wave in Quebec, one of the most active periods of UFO activity in Canadian history.

## The Wave

The Val-d'Or wave began in August 1973 and continued through October, with multiple reports filed each night. Key patterns:

**August 1973:** Initial reports of multi-colored lights appearing over the mining town. Witnesses described objects hovering silently above the town, changing colors between red, white, green, and amber.

**September 1973:** The frequency of sightings increased dramatically. Reports came from multiple towns across the Abitibi-Témiscamingue region. Mining workers on night shift reported objects following them on roads. Police officers filed multiple incident reports.

**October 1973:** The wave peaked with mass sightings involving dozens of witnesses on single nights. The phenomenon began to taper off by late October.

## Object Descriptions

Across the hundreds of reports, consistent descriptions emerged:

- **Shapes:** Primarily luminous orbs and elongated objects; some described as triangular or disc-shaped
- **Colors:** Multi-colored — objects changed between red, white, green, and amber during observation
- **Movement:** Erratic, non-conventional flight patterns — hovering, instant acceleration, right-angle turns
- **Sound:** Most reports described the objects as completely silent
- **Size:** Estimates ranged from small (car-sized) to very large (50+ feet)

## Law Enforcement Involvement

Quebec provincial police (Sûreté du Québec) and local Val-d'Or police received dozens of calls during the wave. Officers on patrol reported seeing the objects themselves and filed official incident reports. The volume of reports was significant enough that Quebec ufology organizations (SATELU and others) began coordinating data collection.

## Regional Context — Quebec 1973

The Val-d'Or wave was part of a broader pattern of UFO activity across Quebec in 1973. Other notable Quebec cases from that year included:

- **Granby, QC (October 1973):** Multi-colored object with apparent entity sighting (CE3)
- **Montreal area:** Multiple reports of luminous objects
- **Eastern Townships:** Rural sightings by farmers

Quebec experienced one of the highest per-capita rates of UFO reports in Canada during this period, and the 1973 wave remains one of the most active documented periods in the province's history.

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Hundreds of witnesses across multiple towns
- Includes trained observers (police, mining supervisors)
- Sustained multi-week activity with consistent descriptions
- Documented by Quebec ufology organizations
- Official police incident reports filed

**Weaknesses:**
- Mass wave cases inherently lack specific physical evidence
- No high-quality photographs from the period
- No military radar data publicly available (though some reports suggest radar contact)
- Media coverage was sensational, which damaged scientific credibility

## Agent Note
The 1973 Quebec wave should be positioned as one of the most significant mass UFO events in Canadian history. It represents the "regional epidemic" pattern seen in other famous waves: the Belgian Triangle wave (1989-90), the Gulf Breeze wave (1987), and the Phoenix Lights (1997). Val-d'Or 1973 is Canada's answer to these — a concentrated, multi-week, multi-witness event that overwhelmed local authorities.
