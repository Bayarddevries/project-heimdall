# CAN-033: St-Zotique 1991 — Quebec Mass Sighting

**Date:** 1991
**Location:** Saint-Zotique, Quebec, Canada (45.23°N, 74.25°W)
**Classification:** NL — Mass Nocturnal Lights Sighting

## Case Summary

In 1991, multiple witnesses in and around Saint-Zotique, Quebec reported observing a formation of unusual lights in the night sky. The sighting is consistent with the continuing pattern of unusual aerial phenomena in the Quebec region, which has been one of Canada's most active areas for UFO reports.

## The Event

Witnesses in Saint-Zotique — a community on the South Shore of the St. Lawrence River, approximately 60 km southwest of Montreal — reported seeing multiple lights in an unusual formation pattern.

**Details:**
- **Lights:** Multiple objects with varying colors, observed in a coordinated formation
- **Pattern:** The formation pattern was described as unusual and not consistent with known aircraft formations
- **Sound:** Silent observation (reported by witnesses)
- **Duration:** Extended observation period
- **Witnesses:** Multiple community members

## Regional Context

Saint-Zotique is located in an area that has seen consistent UFO activity:
- South Shore of the St. Lawrence River valley
- Part of the broader Quebec sighting corridor
- Proximity to the U.S. border (New York state)
- The region has historically produced credible sighting reports

Quebec as a whole has one of the highest per-capita UFO sighting rates in Canada, possibly due to:
- Active local UFO research organizations (GRUPI, MUFON Quebec)
- Cultural acceptance of the phenomenon
- Geographic factors (St. Lawrence River valley as a "corridor")
