# CAN-032: Carp, Ontario — "The Guardian" Landing Case

**Date:** November 4, 1989
**Location:** Rural field near Carp, Ontario, Canada (45.3°N, 76.0°W)
**Classification:** CE3 — Close Encounter of the Third Kind

## Case Summary

In November 1989 — the same month the Berlin Wall fell — Diane Labenek reported a close encounter with a large, dark triangular craft in a rural field near Carp, Ontario, approximately 30 kilometers northwest of Ottawa. The case became one of Canada's most controversial and heavily investigated CE3 encounters, largely due to the involvement of American ufologist Bob Oechsler, who produced documentary footage known as "The Guardian" tapes.

## The Event

On the evening of November 4, 1989, Diane Labenek reported observing a large, dark triangular craft hovering at low altitude over a field near her property in the Carp area. Key details:

- **Shape:** Large triangular/delta-shaped craft, estimated 30-50 feet per side
- **Color:** Dark exterior with red-orange flame-like illumination underneath
- **Altitude:** Extremely low — approximately 10-50 feet above the field
- **Sound:** Described as a low humming or buzzing sound
- **Duration:** Extended duration — witness observed craft for a significant period
- **Entities:** Labenek reported seeing one or more figures near or associated with the craft

## Physical Evidence

After the encounter, Labenek discovered physical evidence in the field:

- **Scorch marks:** Circular or triangular patterns of burned/singed vegetation
- **Soil disturbance:** The ground in the affected area appeared disturbed
- **Vegetation damage:** Crops/grass were flattened and scorched

Photos of the field marks were taken and documented. Soil samples were allegedly collected, though independent laboratory analysis results are unclear.

## "The Guardian" Tapes

Bob Oechsler, an American ufologist and MUFON investigator, traveled to Carp to investigate the case. He conducted interviews with Labenek and produced extensive VHS footage of:

- The encounter site and physical trace evidence
- Labenek's testimony (extended interview)
- The surrounding area and approach routes
- Alleged electromagnetic effects in the area

The "Guardian" tapes became a significant piece of UFO documentary footage. However, they were also controversial — critics noted the production quality and Oechsler's dramatic narration style, which some felt sensationalized the case.

## Context — November 1989

The timing is notable: this encounter occurred during the Belgian UFO Wave (November 1989 – April 1990), one of the most famous mass UFO sighting events in history, involving thousands of witnesses and F-16 intercepts over Belgium. The Carp case shares several features with the Belgian Triangle wave:

- Dark triangular craft
- Low-altitude hovering
- Colored lights (red-orange) at corners
- November 1989 timeframe

## Assessment

The Carp case has both strengths and weaknesses:

**Strengths:**
- Physical trace evidence (scorch marks, field disturbance)
- Professional investigation by MUFON/CFU
- Documentary footage exists
- Witness was consistent in her account over time

**Weaknesses:**
- Single primary witness for the encounter itself
- Oechsler's sensational approach damaged credibility in some circles
- No independent laboratory confirmation of soil samples
- The "Guardian" tapes are more theatrical than scientific
