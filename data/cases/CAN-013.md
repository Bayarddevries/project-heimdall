# CAN-013: 1967 Shelburne, NS — Object Over Atlantic Waters

**Date:** 1967
**Location:** Shelburne, Nova Scotia, Canada (43.7°N, 65.3°W)
**Classification:** CE1 — Close Encounter | Naval Observation

## Case Summary

During 1967 — the most significant year in Canadian UFO history, which included the famous Shag Harbour incident just ~20km north — military personnel operating in the Shelburne, Nova Scotia area observed a disc-shaped object that appeared to enter the Atlantic Ocean. The event was tracked on naval radar.

## Historical Context — 1967: Canada's UFO Year

1967 was arguably the most dramatic year for UFO sightings in Canadian history:
- **October 4, 1967:** Shag Harbour — the most famous UFO crash-recovery operation in Canadian history
- **Throughout 1967:** Nova Scotia's South Shore was a hotspot for sightings
- The Shelburne incident occurred in this same year, in this same region, suggesting the Atlantic coast may have been a zone of activity

## The Event

**Observation Details:**
- **Object:** Disc-shaped
- **Action:** The object was observed entering the water (Atlantic Ocean) from the air
- **Location:** Shelburne NS coastline — Atlantic coastal waters
- **Confirmation:** Tracked on naval radar equipment

## Naval Context

Shelburne is located on the Atlantic coast of Nova Scotia, and Royal Canadian Navy assets operated in this region. The presence of naval radar equipment means the sighting was not just visual — it was instrument-confirmed.

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Military (naval) observers
- Radar confirmation
- Same region/year as the Shag Harbour incident (corroborating activity pattern)
- Instrument + visual confirmation

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited public documentation available
- No physical recovery reported
- Ocean environment makes search/recovery impossible
