# CAN-021: Langenburg - Five Saucers & Crop Formations

**Date:** September 1, 1974
**Location:** Langenburg, Saskatchewan, Canada (50.8°N, 101.4°W)
**Classification:** CE2 — Close Encounter of the Second Kind (Physical Evidence)

## Case Summary

On September 1, 1974, Edwin Fuhr, a farmer in the Langenburg area of Saskatchewan, discovered five metallic saucer-shaped objects hovering over his grain field. After the objects departed, he found five circular rings of flattened and swirled grain where they had been positioned. The case was investigated by the RCMP and remains one of the best-documented ground trace cases in Canadian ufology.

## Initial Observation

Edwin Fuhr was working on his farm when he noticed five metallic, disc-shaped objects hovering at low altitude over his grain field. He described them as:
- Approximately 3-4 feet in diameter each
- Metallic silver/gray in color
- Hovering silently about 2-3 feet above the crops
- Arranged in a rough circular pattern

Fuhr observed the objects for several minutes before they suddenly departed at extremely high speed, disappearing from view in seconds.

## The Ground Trace

After the objects left, Fuhr walked to the field and discovered five distinct circular rings where the grain had been flattened and swirled in a clockwise pattern. Key features:

- Five rings, each approximately 12-15 feet in diameter
- Grain was not broken but bent and swirled at the base
- The pattern was consistent with a downward pressure from above
- The rings corresponded to the positions where Fuhr had seen the objects hovering
- Soil samples from the ring centers showed no abnormal radiation or chemical changes

## RCMP Investigation

The Royal Canadian Mounted Police were called to investigate. Officers from the Langenburg detachment:
- Photographed the rings from ground and aerial perspectives
- Collected soil samples from inside and outside the rings
- Interviewed Edwin Fuhr and documented his testimony
- Forwarded the case file to Canadian Forces Headquarters (CFHQ) UFO desk

The RCMP report was detailed and treated the case seriously as a physical trace investigation.

## Aftermath

Edwin Fuhr became a well-known figure in Canadian ufology. The case was featured in several UFO surveys and publications. His credibility as a simple, honest farmer lent weight to the account.
