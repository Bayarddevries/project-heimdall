# CAN-004: 1950 Rivers, MB — RCAF Pilot Metallic Disc Encounter

**Date:** 1950
**Location:** Rivers, Manitoba, Canada (50.0°N, 100.2°W)
**Classification:** RV — Radar-Visual Confirmation | Military Pilot Encounter

## Case Summary

An RCAF pilot encountered a metallic disc-shaped object in the airspace near Rivers, Manitoba in 1950. The pilot logged the encounter, and radar confirmation was obtained. The object performed maneuvers that were beyond the capabilities of any conventional aircraft of the period.

This predates the most famous UFO wave of 1952 by two years, demonstrating that Canadian military pilots were already making contact with unidentified objects.

## The Encounter

The pilot, operating in the vicinity of Rivers, Manitoba, spotted a metallic disc-shaped object in clear flight conditions. The pilot's log includes:

- **Shape:** Disc, metallic surface reflecting sunlight
- **Movement:** High-speed maneuvering inconsistent with known aircraft
- **Altitude:** Standard flight-level altitude
- **Confirmation:** The object was simultaneously tracked on radar

## Significance

This case is notable because it represents one of the earliest documented military pilot UFO encounters in Canada. The pilot was a trained military aviator, the encounter was radar-confirmed, and it occurred years before the 1952 wave that brought global attention to the phenomenon.
