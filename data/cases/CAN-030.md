# CAN-030: 1989 Shelburne, NS — Triangle from the Deep

**Date:** 1989
**Location:** Shelburne, Nova Scotia, Canada (43.7°N, 65.3°W)
**Classification:** CE1 — Close Encounter | Maritime Triangle Sighting

## Case Summary

In 1989, local fishermen working off the coast of Shelburne, Nova Scotia observed a large triangular craft emerge from the Atlantic Ocean waters. The witnesses were experienced mariners familiar with normal marine and aerial traffic. Their testimony describes a structured, manufactured object transitioning from underwater to aerial operation.

## The Event

**Fishermen's Observations:**
- **Initial contact:** A large triangular shape was observed rising from the water
- **Shape:** Distinctly triangular — the fishermen were confident in their identification
- **Size:** Large craft — substantial enough to be clearly visible and intimidating
- **Sequence:** The object emerged from the water and became airborne
- **Sound:** Descriptions vary on whether engine noise was present

## Maritime Witness Context

The fishermen were experienced observers:
- They knew the Atlantic coastal waters intimately
- They were familiar with normal marine traffic (ships, submarines) and aerial traffic
- They had no reason to misidentify a conventional object
- Multiple fishermen corroborated the account

## Historical Context — Shelburne's 1967 Sighting

Interestingly, Shelburne was also the site of a 1967 UFO incident (CAN-013), where military personnel observed an object entering the water. The 1989 case represents the inverse — an object emerging from the water. This suggests the Shelburne area may be a zone of UAP (Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena) entry and exit activity.

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Experienced maritime witnesses (fishermen)
- Multiple witnesses
- Specific, detailed description (triangular shape; emergence from water)
- Prior UFO activity in the same area (1967 entry event)

**Weaknesses:**
- No photographs or video
- No radar confirmation
- No physical evidence recovered

## Agent Notes
The Shelburne entry-exit pattern (object enters water in 1967, object emerges in 1989) is one of the most intriguing location-based patterns in Canadian UFO history. The triangular shape connects to the broader triangle craft theme seen in other Canadian cases (Saddle Lake 1979, etc.).
