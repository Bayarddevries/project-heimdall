# CAN-003: Goose Bay 1947 — Military Radar Anomaly

**Date:** 1947
**Location:** Goose Bay, Newfoundland, Canada (CFB Goose Bay) (53.3°N, 60.4°W)
**Classification:** RV — Radar Detection | Military Intelligence

## Case Summary

In 1947 — the same year as Kenneth Arnold's famous Mount Rainier sighting that coined the term "flying saucer" — military radar operators at CFB Goose Bay detected an unidentified object moving at speeds exceeding 9,000 miles per hour. This represents one of the earliest radar-documented UFO cases in North America and demonstrates that aerial anomalies were being detected by military equipment from the very beginning of the modern UFO era.

## Historical Context — 1947

1947 is a landmark year in UFO history:
- **June 24, 1947:** Kenneth Arnold sighted nine "flying saucers" near Mount Rainier, Washington
- **July 8, 1947:** The Roswell incident ( debris recovered in New Mexico)
- **Throughout 1947:** Hundreds of civilian and military reports across North America

The Goose Bay sighting occurred against this backdrop of intense UFO activity. Military installations were on heightened alert, and radar operators were trained to distinguish between conventional aircraft and anomalies.

## CFB Goose Bay

CFB (Canadian Forces Base) Goose Bay was — and remains — one of the most strategically important military installations in Canada:

- Located in Labrador, it controlled air access to the North Atlantic
- Served as a critical refueling and monitoring station for transatlantic flights
- Was part of the early North American air defense network (pre-NORAD predecessor systems)
- Had advanced radar equipment for the time

## The Radar Contact

Military radar operators at Goose Bay detected an object moving at speeds in excess of **9,000 miles per hour** — an impossible velocity for any known aircraft in 1947.

For context:
- The fastest aircraft of 1947 (the Bell X-1) had just broken the sound barrier (~760 mph)
- Commercial airliners cruised at around 200-300 mph
- V-2 rockets reached approximately 3,500 mph
- 9,000+ mph exceeded the capabilities of any known vehicle

The radar contact was brief but definitive. The object registered on military radar equipment, providing an objective, instrument-based measurement independent of human visual perception.

## USAF Intelligence Documentation

The case was documented in USAF Intelligence files, which were later declassified. The involvement of USAF Intelligence indicates that the Goose Bay detection was considered significant enough to warrant attention from U.S. military intelligence — not just a routine radar blip.

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Instrument-based detection (radar) — objective, non-subjective evidence
- Military radar operators (trained technicians)
- Documented in official USAF Intelligence files
- Occurred at a strategically critical military installation
- Speed measurement (9,000+ mph) rules out conventional aircraft

**Weaknesses:**
- No visual corroboration reported
- Single radar installation (no triangulation)
- Limited details available in declassified files
- Could theoretically be a radar anomaly, though the documented speed makes this unlikely

## Agent Note
The 1947 Goose Bay case is foundational. It's one of the earliest radar-documented UFO cases worldwide, and the 9,000+ mph speed exceeds anything that was technologically possible at the time. This case should be positioned alongside the 1947 Mantell incident (Kentucky) and the Chiles-Whitted encounter (July 1948) as early, credible, instrument-confirmed anomalies.
