# CAN-018: Duncan Hospital UFO Encounter
**Date:** January 1, 1970 (shortly after midnight)
**Location:** Cowichan District Hospital, Duncan, British Columbia (48.7°N, 123.7°W)
**Classification:** CE3 — Close Encounter of the Third Kind (Beings Observed)

## Case Summary
On New Year's Day 1970, nurse Doreen Kendall and a colleague witnessed a luminous craft land in a field adjacent to the Cowichan District Hospital parking lot. Two tall, humanoid entities emerged and approached them. Kendall reported an overwhelming psychological reaction — paralysis and unnatural dread — as the beings moved toward them in a stiff, synchronized, gliding manner. The encounter lasted approximately 3-5 minutes before the entities returned to their craft and departed rapidly.

Physical trace evidence was documented the following day at the landing site.

This case predates the more famous Pascagoula (1973) and Travis Walton (1975) incidents by several years, making it one of the earliest documented hospital-close-encounter cases in North American ufology.

## Detailed Narrative

### The Encounter

Shortly after midnight on January 1, 1970, Doreen Kendall was working the night shift at Cowichan District Hospital in Duncan, BC. Duncan is located on Vancouver Island, roughly midway between Victoria and Nanaimo.

After finishing her shift, Kendall and a fellow nurse colleague walked through the hospital parking lot to head to their vehicles. The area was quiet, typical of a hospital lot in the early morning hours of a holiday.

As the two nurses approached their car, they noticed an unusual illumination in the sky. A large, silent craft descended toward a field adjacent to the hospital parking lot. The object emitted a bright, otherworldly glow that illuminated the surrounding area.

Kendall later recounted that as the craft touched down, two figures emerged from it and began walking toward them. She experienced an overwhelming psychological and physiological reaction to the entities' presence:

> "I couldn't move. I was just paralyzed with fear. It wasn't just normal fear; it felt like something external was holding me there."

> "I was fully conscious and retained a sharp, detailed visual memory of the beings and the craft until the entities returned to their vehicle and departed rapidly."

### Description of the Entities

Kendall reported that the beings were humanoid but distinctly non-human in proportion and demeanor:

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Height | Approximately 7 feet tall or taller |
| Attire | Dark, form-fitting, one-piece suits without visible seams, buttons, or insignia; material appeared smooth and metallic or synthetic |
| Eyes | Unusually large, dark, elongated |
| Facial features | Small or indistinct; no prominent nose or mouth discernible |
| Movement | Stiff, synchronized, gliding rather than natural bipedal gait |

> "They didn't walk like humans. They seemed to glide, and they were unnaturally tall."

### Physical Evidence

The day following the encounter, Kendall and interested parties returned to the landing site near the hospital. Investigators documented several physical anomalies consistent with the nurses' timeline:

**Ground Traces:**
- A distinct triangular or circular pattern was found in the soil/grass where the craft was reported to have touched down
- The ground within the pattern was unusually dry, hard, and possibly scorched, contrasting with the damp, frozen Vancouver Island winter soil surrounding it
- Grass/ground cover in the immediate area appeared flattened or chemically altered — a common signature in high-strangeness landing cases

### Police Response

The incident was reported to the local Duncan RCMP detachment. Police officers visited the scene and interviewed the witnesses. While official police reports from the era generally concluded with standard language, officers reportedly noted that:
- Witnesses were coherent
- Witnesses were non-intoxicated
- Witnesses were not prone to fantasy

### Witness Credibility

**Doreen Kendall:**
- Respected, professional nurse at Cowichan District Hospital
- No history of psychological instability, substance abuse, or prior UFO claims
- Calm, steady demeanor under questioning
- Willing to be publicly identified in UFO research circles
- Reportedly underwent polygraph testing by independent investigators of the era and passed

The identity of the second nurse witness remains largely unreported in public archives to protect the privacy of non-public figures. Doreen Kendall is the primary named source in all public records.

### Media Coverage

The story initially broke in local Vancouver Island newspapers (Victoria Times-Colonist and local Duncan papers) shortly after the event, often framed cautiously.

Over the decades, it has been featured in:
- Canadian UFO documentaries
- UFO literature focusing on West Coast close encounters
- Chris Rutkowski's Canadian UFO Surveys (British Columbia volumes)
- CUFON case database

The case remains a cornerstone example of a "hospital parking lot" abduction/encounter case in Canadian ufology.

## Classification Assessment

**Hynek System:** CE3 — Close Encounter of the Third Kind (beings observed at close range)

**Credibility Rating: HIGH**

Factors supporting high credibility:
1. Primary witness is a medical professional (nurse) with no UFO history
2. Second corroborating witness (unnamed nurse colleague)
3. Physical trace evidence documented at the landing site
4. Police investigation with positive witness assessment
5. Reported polygraph passed
6. Willingness to be publicly identified

**Case Status:** UNRESOLVED — No conventional explanation accounts for the witness testimony, physical evidence, and rapid departure of the craft.

## Case Significance

The Duncan Hospital case is one of the most credible CE3 encounters in Canadian history because:
- The witness was a trained medical professional
- The encounter occurred in a familiar, well-lit environment (hospital parking lot)
- Physical evidence was collected and documented
- The witness passed investigative scrutiny
- It predates the more famous American CE3 cases by several years
