# CAN-036: 1994 St-Jean-sur-Richelieu, QC — Trace Case with Dehydrated Vegetation

**Date:** 1994
**Location:** Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu, Quebec, Canada (45.3°N, 73.2°W)
**Classification:** CE2 — Close Encounter of the Second Kind (Physical Trace Evidence)

## Case Summary

In 1994, a local farmer in Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu, Quebec discovered physical evidence of a UFO landing on his property. An oval craft left behind dehydrated vegetation and ground marks that were documented and analyzed.

## The Evidence

**Physical traces found:**
- **Dehydrated vegetation:** Plants in a specific area showed signs of severe dehydration — moisture stripped from the vegetation in a pattern consistent with intense heat or radiation exposure
- **Ground marks:** Circular or oval-shaped impressions in the ground, consistent with a craft landing or hovering at close proximity
- **Soil effects:** Soil chemistry may have been altered (common in trace cases)

## Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu

Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu sits in the Richelieu Valley, south of Montreal:
- Agricultural region — farmland with crops that show damage clearly
- Rich history of UFO activity in the Quebec corridor
- Close to the U.S. border (Vermont/New York state)

## Trace Case Analysis

The physical evidence was examined by UFO researchers:
- The dehydration pattern was unusual — not consistent with fire, chemical spray, or natural causes
- The ground marks suggested a physical object of substantial weight and defined shape (oval)
- The combination of ground marks and vegetation damage is characteristic of close-proximity UFO landings

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- **Physical evidence** — objective, measurable traces
- Agricultural setting makes unusual vegetation damage conspicuous
- Farmer witness (familiar with normal agricultural conditions)
- Ground marks + vegetation damage (dual trace categories)
- Documented case with photographic evidence of the trace site

**Weaknesses:**
- No photograph of the craft itself
- No radar confirmation
- Trace evidence degrades over time

## Classification
- Hynek System: CE2 (Close Encounter - Physical Evidence)
- Credibility Rating: HIGH (physical trace evidence is objective; agricultural expert witness)
- Case Status: UNRESOLVED — No conventional source for the combined ground marks and vegetation dehydration
