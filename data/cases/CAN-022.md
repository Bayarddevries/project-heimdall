# CAN-022: 1975 Carman, MB — Charlie Redstar

**Date:** 1975-1976
**Location:** Carman, Manitoba, Canada (49.5°N, 98.0°W) — observed across central Canada
**Classification:** NL — Nocturnal Lights | Mass Sighting Phenomenon

## Case Summary

Beginning in 1975, a mysterious red pulsating light — dubbed "Charlie Redstar" by investigators — was observed by thousands of people across Manitoba and central Canada. The phenomenon recurred over months, generated hundreds of reports, and was documented by a dedicated investigative team led by Charlie Redstar.

## Charlie Redstar

Charlie Redstar (also known as Grant) was a Manitoba resident who took on the role of primary investigator and chronicler of this phenomenon:
- He logged sightings, interviewed witnesses, and conducted field research
- He became the central figure in documenting and investigating this specific phenomenon
- He maintained files on the Red Light sightings from 1975 onward

## The Phenomenon

**Characteristics:**
- **Appearance:** A bright red, pulsating light visible in the night sky
- **Behavior:** Appeared and disappeared; sometimes seemed to track along paths; often observed near railway lines
- **Recurrence:** The phenomenon was observed repeatedly over months, sometimes multiple times per night
- **Scale:** Thousands of sightings across Manitoba and parts of central Canada

## Community Impact

The Charlie Redstar phenomenon:
- Generated widespread community interest and concern
- Was covered by Manitoba newspapers (including the Winnipeg Free Press)
- Created a "sighting culture" — people actively looked for and reported the light
- Brought together a community of witnesses and investigators

## Conventional Explanations Considered
- **Aircraft navigation lights:** Red lights are used on aircraft, but the scale and behavior didn't match
- **Advertising beacons:** No known advertising campaign matched the pattern
- **Weather balloon:** No evidence of balloon launches
- **Satellite:** No known satellite produced a red point of light visible from Manitoba
- The Charlie Redstar (Grant) investigation itself found no conventional explanation

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Thousands of witnesses
- Sustained phenomenon over months
- Dedicated investigation (Charlie Redstar/Grant)
- Newspaper documentation

**Weaknesses:**
- No physical evidence
- Community excitement may have influenced some reports
- No radar confirmation
