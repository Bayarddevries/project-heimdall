# CAN-024: Montreal 1977 — Florida Malboeuf Saucer Encounter

**Date:** 1977
**Location:** Montreal, Quebec, Canada (45.5°N, 73.6°W)
**Classification:** CE2 — Close Encounter of the Second Kind (Physical Evidence)

## Case Summary

In 1977, Florida Malboeuf reported a close encounter with a landed saucer in the Montreal area that left physical trace evidence in the form of landing gear marks in snow. This case was investigated by Quebec ufology researchers and added to the Canadian UFO Survey archives.

Quebec has one of the richest UFO case histories in Canada, and this encounter represents one of several notable CE2 cases from the province in the 1970s.

## The Event

Florida Malboeuf reported observing a saucer-shaped craft that had landed in the Montreal area. The snow-covered ground at the time of the encounter provided an ideal medium for preserving physical evidence.

After the craft departed, Malboeuf discovered distinct landing gear impressions in the snow — marks left by whatever structure supported the craft while it was on the ground. These impressions were photographed and documented by Quebec ufology researchers.

## Physical Evidence — Landing Gear Marks

The landing gear marks are the primary physical evidence in this case. Snow is an excellent medium for preserving trace evidence because:

- Impressions are clearly visible against undisturbed snow
- Marks can be photographed and measured
- The pattern of marks can reveal the landing configuration (number of "legs," spacing, pressure distribution)

The marks were consistent with a multi-point landing configuration, suggesting the craft had some form of landing gear or support structure.

## Quebec UFO Research Context

Quebec had an active ufology research community in the 1970s, with several organizations and individual researchers documenting cases across the province. Project Logos and other Quebec-based investigations contributed significantly to Canada's UFO case history.

## Assessment

**Weaknesses:**
