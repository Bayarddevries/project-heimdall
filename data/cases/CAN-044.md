# CAN-044: 2015 Oshawa, ON — The Black Cube

**Date:** 2015
**Location:** Oshawa, Ontario, Canada (43.8°N, 78.8°W)
**Classification:** DD — Daylight Disc | Video Evidence

## Case Summary

In 2015, an amateur videographer in Oshawa, Ontario captured video footage of a black cube-shaped object rotating in the sky above the city. The video was uploaded to the internet and became one of the most well-known Canadian UFO videos due to the unmistakable geometric shape of the object.

## The Video

**What the footage shows:**
- **Object:** A black cube (hexahedron) — unmistakably geometric
- **Behavior:** Rotating/spinning in clear daylight sky
- **Background:** Clear sky; well-lit conditions
- **Resolution:** Clear enough to identify the cube shape

A cube is one of the least ambiguous shapes a UFO can have — unlike orbs or lights, a cube cannot be explained as a lens flare, planet, or weather balloon.

## Oshawa, ON

Oshawa is part of the Greater Toronto Area (Durham Region):
- Approximately 60 km east of downtown Toronto
- Urban environment with significant light pollution — yet the object was clearly visible
- No military presence that would explain a cube-shaped aircraft

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- **Video evidence** — objective documentation
- **Daylight conditions** — eliminated many common explanations
- **Cube shape** — unambiguous geometry; no natural phenomenon produces perfect cubes
- **Rotation** — controlled, deliberate movement

**Weaknesses:**
- Video resolution and distance limit precise analysis
- No radar data
- Amateur recording (no professional equipment)

## Cubes as a UFO Sub-Type

Black cube UFOs are a recognized sub-category:
- Similar cube-shaped objects have been reported worldwide
- The geometric precision of the shape suggests manufactured origin
- Some researchers link cube sightings to drone-like UAP or structured craft

## Classification
- Hynek System: DD (Daylight Disc)
- Credibility Rating: HIGH (daylight video; unmistakable cube shape)
- Case Status: UNRESOLVED — No conventional explanation for a rotating black cube in the Oshawa sky
