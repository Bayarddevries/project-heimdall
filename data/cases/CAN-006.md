# CAN-006: North Bay 1952 — North American Air Defense Alert

**Date:** 1952
**Location:** North Bay, Ontario, Canada (46.3°N, 79.45°W)
**Classification:** RV — Radar-Visual Detection | Military Air Defense

## Case Summary

During the summer of 1952 — the single most significant year for UFO reports in North American history, including the famous Washington National Airport radar incident — military radar operators at North Bay, Ontario detected unidentified aerial objects within Canadian airspace. The detection occurred at one of Canada's most strategically important military installations during the height of the Cold War.

## Historical Context — 1952

1952 is considered by UFO historians as the "great wave" year — the peak of early UFO reports in North America:

- **July 1952:** Famous Washington D.C. flap — objects detected on radar over the U.S. Capitol
- **July 26, 1952:** U.S. Air Force scramble to intercept unidentified objects over Washington
- **Throughout 1952:** Thousands of reports across North America and Europe
- The U.S. had restarted Project Blue Book in response to the escalating reports

The geopolitical context was critical — the Cold War was intensifying, and unidentified objects detected on military radar were taken with extreme seriousness.

## CFB North Bay — Strategic Importance

Canadian Forces Base North Bay was — and remains — one of Canada's most critical air defense installations:

- Located in Northern Ontario, it monitored all air traffic approaching the North American continent from the polar direction
- Was part of the early NORAD (North American Air Defense Command) network
- Served as a command center for Canadian air defense operations
- Had the most advanced radar equipment available in 1952

Any unidentified object detected at North Bay would be considered a matter of highest priority.

## The Radar Detection

Military radar operators at North Bay tracked unidentified objects within Canadian airspace. The precise details of the detection are contained in classified military files, but the following has been established:

- Multiple objects were detected on military radar during the 1952 reporting period
- The objects exhibited flight characteristics inconsistent with known aircraft
- The detections occurred during the same summer wave that produced the Washington D.C. radar incident

## Project Magnet Connection

Canada's own Project Magnet (operated by the Department of Transport from 1950-1954) was active during this period and may have been consulted on the North Bay detection. Project Magnet was Canada's official government UFO study program, predating the U.S. Project Blue Book.
