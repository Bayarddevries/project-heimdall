# CAN-026: 1979 Saddle Lake, AB — First Nations Triangle Sighting

**Date:** 1979
**Location:** Saddle Lake, Alberta, Canada (53.9°N, 111.6°W)
**Classification:** CE1 — Close Encounter | Triangle Craft

## Case Summary

In 1979, members of the Saddle Lake First Nations community in Alberta observed a large triangular craft flying silently at low altitude over their territory. The sighting was documented in community logs and represents one of several First Nations UFO observations recorded in Canadian history.

## Cultural Context

First Nations communities across Canada have a rich oral tradition of "star people" and sky beings. Modern UFO sightings by First Nations observers are sometimes interpreted within this cultural context:

- Saddle Lake is a Cree First Nation in central Alberta
- The community has strong ties to the land and sky observation
- Triangular craft sightings by First Nations are significant — the triangle motif appears in Indigenous cosmologies

## The Event

**Observation Details:**
- **Shape:** Large triangle
- **Movement:** Silent flight at low altitude
- **Visibility:** Close enough to clearly identify the triangular shape
- **Sound:** Silent — no engine noise
- **Context:** Observed by multiple community members
