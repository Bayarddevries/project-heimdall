# CAN-025: 1978 Baskatong, QC — Jacques Lavoie's Saucer Photograph

**Date:** 1978
**Location:** Baskatong, Quebec, Canada (46.8°N, 75.9°W)
**Classification:** DD — Daylight Disc | Photographic Evidence

## Case Summary

In 1978, Jacques Lavoie of Baskatong, Quebec captured a photograph of a saucer-shaped object on 35mm film. The photograph was taken in daylight conditions, making it one of the more significant photographic pieces of evidence in Canadian UFO history.

## The Photograph

**Details:**
- **Medium:** 35mm film — a common, well-understood photographic format
- **Conditions:** Daylight — the saucer shape is clearly visible
- **Quality:** Described as "detailed" — the photograph captured enough detail to identify the object as a saucer
- **Analysis:** The photograph was analyzed and deemed not to be a hoax or manipulation

## Jacques Lavoie

Lavoie was a Quebec resident who happened to capture this photograph. He cooperated with investigators and provided the original film for analysis.

## Photographic Assessment

The Baskatong photograph was analyzed by UFO researchers:
- The saucer shape was clearly identifiable
- No evidence of double exposure or manipulation
- The daylight conditions eliminated many common explanations (lens flare, light reflection)
- The object appeared to be a solid craft

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- 35mm photographic evidence
- Daylight conditions
- Analyzed and deemed authentic by researchers
- Single-witness but with physical evidence (photograph)

**Weaknesses:**
- Resolution and clarity limitations of 35mm photography
- Only available for analysis
