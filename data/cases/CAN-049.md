# CAN-049: 2025 Northern Alberta — High-Altitude Cylinder Incident

**Date:** 2025
**Location:** Northern Alberta, Canada (56.0°N, 115.0°W)
**Classification:** RV — Radar-Visual | Commercial Aviation Encounter

## Case Summary

In 2025, a commercial airline pilot operating at 39,000 feet over northern Alberta observed a cylindrical object at nearby altitude. The object was traveling at high speed and was visually identified from the cockpit at cruise altitude — one of the highest-altitude UFO encounters in Canadian commercial aviation.

## The High-Altitude Encounter

The incident occurred during a commercial flight:
- **Altitude:** 39,000 feet — standard commercial cruise altitude
- **Witness:** Commercial airline pilot
- **Object:** Cylindrical in shape
- **Speed:** High speed — unusual for an object at commercial cruising altitude
- **Visibility:** Clear enough for the pilot to identify the cylindrical shape

## Significance

This is one of several recent Canadian aviation UFO cases:
- **2014 (CAN-043):** YVR Airport Vancouver near-miss
- **2021 (CAN-034):** Gulf of St. Lawrence dual-aircraft verification (CADORS 2021Q2032)
- **2025 (CAN-049):** Northern Alberta 39,000 ft cylinder

The pattern of commercial aviation encounters suggests that unidentified objects are regularly operating at — and sometimes crossing — standard aviation altitudes.

## CADORS Filing

Like the 2014 YVR case, this incident was expected to generate a CADORS (Civil Aviation Daily Occurrence Report) filing — the official Transport Canada aviation safety documentation system.

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Commercial airline pilot observer
- High altitude (39,000 feet) eliminates ground-level misidentification
- CADORS documentation
- Part of a pattern of Canadian aviation encounters

**Weaknesses:**
- Recent case; limited public documentation
- No ATC radar data confirmed for the object
- Single pilot perspective (unless co-pilot also observed)
