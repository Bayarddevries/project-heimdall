# CAN-051: Chinese Spy Balloon - Surfside Beach Shootdown
**Date:** February 4, 2023 (tracking began January 28, 2023)
**Location:** Surfside Beach, South Carolina (shot down 6 nautical miles offshore, Atlantic Ocean)
**Classification:** CE1 — Military Engagement / Confirmed State Surveillance Platform

## Case Summary
On February 4, 2023, two F-22 Raptor fighter jets shot down a People's Republic of China (PRC) high-altitude surveillance balloon off the coast of South Carolina. The balloon had been tracked by NORAD for seven days as it crossed the Pacific, entered North American airspace over Alaska, drifted over the Yukon and British Columbia, crossed the United States over nuclear missile silos and Strategic Command, and emerged over the Atlantic. The incident triggered an unprecedented geopolitical crisis, sparked massive media coverage, and was followed within days by three additional UFO shootdowns over Alaska, the Yukon, and Lake Huron — suggesting the Chinese balloon incident had prompted a recalibration of NORAD radar systems that revealed previously undetected objects.

This is the first event in the **February 2023 North America Flap** — four objects shot down over North American airspace in eight days.

## Complete Timeline

### Phase 1: Detection and Tracking (January 28 - February 1)

**January 28, 2023** — The object is first detected over the Pacific Ocean by NORAD's layered detection system (Over-the-Horizon radars, space-based infrared sensors, satellite tracking). US Northern Command (NORTHCOM) and broader intelligence assets begin continuous tracking. The White House is briefed; President Biden is informed and presented with military options.

**January 29 (Morning)** — The balloon enters North American airspace over the Aleutian Islands, Alaska.

**January 31** — The balloon crosses the Canadian border, drifting over the Yukon and British Columbia. Canadian officials and the Royal Canadian Air Force (RCAF) monitor its progress. It is not intercepted over Canadian territory.

**February 1 (1:35 PM MT)** — The balloon re-enters US airspace over northern Montana, passing near **Malmstrom Air Force Base** — a Minuteman III nuclear missile base. The Pentagon confirms it is tracking a "high-altitude surveillance balloon."

President Biden explicitly orders the military **NOT** to shoot the balloon down over land. White House Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre: Biden instructed advisors to ensure there was "no risk to Americans on the ground."

### Phase 2: Transit Across the United States (February 2-3)

**February 2** — The balloon drifts southeast across the US. Brig. Gen. Pat Ryder (Pentagon Press Secretary) holds a press briefing, confirming the object is not a threat to people on the ground and is being tracked continuously. Internal discussions focus on *when* to shoot it down.

**February 3** — The balloon crosses the Midwest, passing over Kansas, Missouri, Illinois, and Kentucky. It flies over **Offutt Air Force Base** in Nebraska — home of US Strategic Command. Civilians in Missouri and Illinois capture viral video of the object.

Republican lawmakers heavily criticize the Biden administration for not shooting it down immediately. The White House defends the delay: shooting a 200-foot object with a rigid payload over populated areas risked catastrophic collateral damage.

### Phase 3: Engagement (February 4)

**Morning** — The balloon crosses the Carolinas and reaches the Atlantic Ocean off the coast of South Carolina. The window for a safe maritime intercept opens.

**~2:39 PM EST** — Two F-22A Raptor 5th-generation stealth fighters from the 94th Fighter Squadron ("Hat in the Ring"), 1st Fighter Wing, Joint Base Langley-Eustis, Virginia, intercept the balloon approximately 6 nautical miles south of Surfside Beach, South Carolina. They are supported by a KC-135 Stratotanker for mid-air refueling.

One F-22 fires a single **AIM-9X Sidewinder Block II** infrared-homing missile. Because the balloon lacked a hot jet exhaust, the pilot utilized the missile's advanced imaging infrared (IIR) seeker to lock onto the thermal/visual signature of the payload structure and solar arrays.

The missile strikes the balloon's envelope and payload, venting the helium and causing immediate, catastrophic structural failure. The object plummets into the Atlantic.

### Phase 4: Recovery (February 5-14)

**February 5-9** — The US Navy and Coast Guard launch a joint marine debris recovery operation. The crash site is at approximately 8,000 feet depth. The USCGC *Stone* and Navy assets, including Military Sealift Command vessels and specialized recovery barges, deploy to the site. Navy divers and remotely operated vehicles (ROVs) work in difficult conditions (high seas, 18-knot winds).

**February 9** — The Pentagon announces that "significant" debris has been recovered, including the **main payload module**, solar arrays, sensors, and antennas.

**February 14** — The DoD holds a detailed intelligence briefing showing photos of the recovered payload. Intelligence agencies confirm it was a PRC surveillance balloon equipped with **SIGINT (Signals Intelligence)** collection equipment. The solar arrays proved it was a multi-day, long-endurance platform. No biological, chemical, or nuclear weapons were found.

## Diplomatic Fallout

**Chinese Response:**
- Ministry of Foreign Affairs claimed it was a "civilian airship used primarily for meteorological and other scientific experiments" that "deviated from its planned course due to force majeure" (unforeseeable circumstances, i.e., high winds)
- Feb 4: MFA spokesperson Mao Ning: "China firmly opposes and reserves the right to take further actions regarding the US shootdown of the Chinese civilian airship"
- Feb 5: MFA spokesperson Wang Wenbin called the US action an "overreaction" and "serious violation of international practice," demanding debris return
- China never fully conceded it was spying, though acknowledged it passed near nuclear missile silos

**US-China Diplomatic Crisis:**
- Secretary of State Antony Blinken's scheduled Beijing visit (first since 2018) was cancelled
- The visit was rescheduled and took place in June 2023
- Munich Security Conference (Feb 18): Wang Yi and Blinken had an unplanned, tense 50-minute meeting. Wang Yi demanded an apology; Blinken refused

## Political Reaction

**Republicans:** Criticized the administration for not shooting the balloon down immediately over Montana or Idaho. Accused the White House of appearing "soft on China" and allowing it to spy on US nuclear sites.

**Democrats/White House:** Defended the delay. Argued that shooting a massive, cable-laden payload over populated areas risked crushing homes and injuring civilians. The timeline proved the balloon did not transmit actionable tactical intelligence fast enough to warrant risking American lives.

## Public Reaction

The incident spawned an unprecedented media frenzy. As the balloon crossed the Midwest, millions captured viral videos and photos of the white blob in the sky. Social media was flooded with memes and speculation. News networks interrupted programming with live coverage of the F-22 intercept.

The balloon's cross-America transit became one of the most-watched aerial events in history — millions literally watched it drift across their sky in real time.
