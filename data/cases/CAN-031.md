# CAN-031: November 7, 1990 Montreal — Place Bonaventure Mass Sighting

**Date:** November 7, 1990
**Location:** Montreal, Quebec, Canada (Place Bonaventure area) (45.5°N, 73.5°W)
**Classification:** CE1 — Close Encounter | Mass Urban Sighting

## Case Summary

On November 7, 1990, a luminous oval object hovered over the Place Bonaventure building in downtown Montreal for approximately 3 hours. The event was observed by 40+ people, including Montreal Police officers who filed an official report. The sighting occurred in the heart of Canada's second-largest city, making it one of the most publicly witnessed UFO events in Canadian history.

## The Event

**Sequence of Events:**
- **20:00 (8:00 PM):** Witnesses first noticed a luminous oval object hovering above Place Bonaventure
- **20:00-23:00:** The object remained visible for approximately 3 hours
- **Behavior:** Stationary hover most of the time, with occasional slow, deliberate movements
- **Appearance:** Luminous; bright white/yellowish light; oval shape

## Witness Pool

The diverse witness pool adds significant credibility:
- **Montreal Police officers:** Multiple officers observed and reported; official police report filed
- **Place Bonaventure staff:** Building security and employees witnessed the event
- **Pedestrians:** People in downtown Montreal looked up and observed the anomaly
- **Total:** 40+ witnesses across the urban area

## Place Bonaventure Context

Place Bonaventure is:
- The world's largest exhibition center at the time
- A landmark building in downtown Montreal
- Located near Montreal-Bonaventure station
- Highly visible from multiple vantage points across downtown

## Police Response

Montreal Police took the reports seriously:
- Officers confirmed the observation independently
- An official incident report was filed (November 7, 1990)
- The report documented the witness statements and the object's behavior

## Montreal's UFO History

This incident is part of Montreal's rich UFO history:
- **1662:** Fiery serpents sighting (CAN-001) — the earliest
- **1977:** Florida Malboeuf landing gear marks in snow (CAN-024)
- **1990:** Place Bonaventure mass sighting (this case)

Montreal generates a disproportionate number of UFO reports for a Canadian city.

## Conventional Explanations Considered
- **Aircraft:** Unlikely — 3-hour stationary hover over downtown Montreal would have generated ATC records and would be highly unusual
- **Helicopter:** Would have been noisy and would have required authorization
- **Advertising blimp:** No advertising company claimed the event
- **Weather balloon:** Shape (oval, luminous) and behavior (3-hour hover) inconsistent
- **Drone:** Too early (1990) for consumer drones

## Assessment

**Strengths:**
- 40+ witnesses in urban environment
- Police report and documentation
- 3-hour sustained observation
- Multiple vantage points across downtown Montreal
- Police officers as witnesses (credible, trained observers)

**Weaknesses:**
- No high-quality photographs available publicly
- No radar data confirmed
- No follow-up investigation from authorities
